Hart Family Heirloom Pancakes

Description

Family heirloom pancakes ... Chuck, James, Maximilian, and now Bekah's modifications, too.

Ingredients

  • 5 cups Flour
  • 8 tsp Calumet Baking Powder
  • 3.5 Tbsp Sugar or Honey (requires a smidge more than 3.5T, but is good for color)
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon and/or Vanilla (optional)
  • 3 Eggs
  • 3 Tbsp Olive Oil
  • 4 cups Milk, or 4 cups Buttermilk + 1/2 cup Milk
  • 4-5 cups Apples, Blueberries, Bananas, Chopped Nuts, or anything else you want in your pancakes

Steps

  1. Beat eggs first. Blend in oil. Next mix in milk and then apples (or other additions)
  2. Sift dry ingredients
  3. Fold liquid with apples into flour mix. Stir just to mix. Do not overmix because it makes the pancakes rubbery.
  4. Let sit for 5 minutes or so before spooning out. Break any remaining lumps with the back of the spoon.
  5. Pre-heat heavy pan to 350-375 (water drops dance). Season pan if needed.
  6. Drop from long-handled serving spoon. Turn when sides skinned over and most of the bubbles pop. Peek under edge. If too light, raise heat. If too dark, lower heat. (1/2 number settings on electric stove, or 25 degrees on skillet.)
  7. Cool on rack - they will get soggy if stacked too soon.
  8. Freeze leftovers in pairs in ziploc bags. Pop frozen into toaster on light setting or frozen into microwave for 30 seconds. Reheat 20 seconds if thawed.
